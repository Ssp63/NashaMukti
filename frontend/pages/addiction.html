Understanding Addiction

Learn about the causes, effects, and science of addiction

What is Addiction?

Addiction is a complex, chronic brain disorder characterized by compulsive drug or behavior seeking and use despite harmful consequences. It is considered both a complex brain disorder and a mental illness that affects the brain's reward, motivation, memory, and related circuitry.

People with addiction have an intense focus on using a certain substance(s) or engaging in certain behaviors to the point where it takes over their life. They keep using alcohol or a drug even when they know it will cause problems.

Types of Addiction

Alcohol

Alcohol addiction, also known as alcoholism, is a chronic disease characterized by uncontrolled drinking and preoccupation with alcohol.

Learn More

Drugs

Drug addiction is a chronic disease characterized by compulsive, or uncontrollable, drug seeking and use despite harmful consequences.

Learn More

Tobacco

Tobacco addiction is a dependence on tobacco products caused by the drug nicotine. It can lead to serious health problems and challenges in quitting.

Learn More

Behavioral

Behavioral addictions involve compulsive engagement in rewarding non-substance-related behaviors, like gambling, internet use, gaming, etc.

Causes of Addiction

Addiction is a complex condition with many factors contributing to its development. While no single factor can predict if a person will develop an addiction, the more risk factors present, the greater the likelihood. Here are the key factors that can contribute to addiction:

Genetics

Genetics account for about 40-60% of a person's vulnerability to addiction.

Brain Structure

Certain brain characteristics may make someone more vulnerable to addictive substances.

Environment

Family dynamics, peer pressure, and community factors influence addiction risk.

Early Use

People who begin using substances during adolescence are more likely to develop addiction.

Mental Health

Co-occurring mental health disorders can increase vulnerability to addiction.

Trauma

Physical or emotional trauma can increase risk of substance use as a coping mechanism.
