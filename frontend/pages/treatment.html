Treatment Options

Learn about various approaches to addiction treatment and recovery

Effective Addiction Treatment

Addiction is a complex but treatable condition. Research shows that combining behavioral therapy with medications, where available, is the most effective way to ensure success for most patients. The best programs provide a combination of therapies and other services to meet each patient's needs.

Treatment varies depending on the type of addiction and individual needs, but generally follows a continuum of care. This might include detoxification, rehabilitation, and ongoing recovery support.

Treatment Approaches

Detoxification

Detoxification, or detox, is the process of allowing the body to remove the drugs while managing the symptoms of withdrawal. It is often the first step in a treatment program and is followed by other forms of therapy.

Learn More

Behavioral Therapy

Behavioral therapies help patients modify their attitudes and behaviors related to drug use, increase healthy life skills, and persist with other forms of treatment, such as medication.

Learn More

Medication

Medications can be used to manage withdrawal symptoms, prevent relapse, and treat co-occurring conditions. Different types of medications may be useful at different stages of treatment.

Learn More

Support Groups

Self-help groups can complement and extend the effects of professional treatment. These groups often use the 12-step model and provide peer support to maintain abstinence.

Learn More

Treatment Settings

Treatment can occur in a variety of settings, in many different forms, and for different lengths of time. The specific setting depends on the patient's needs, the types of substances used, and the severity of addiction.

Inpatient/Residential

Patients live in treatment facilities with 24-hour medical and emotional support. Ideal for severe addictions or those with co-occurring disorders.

Outpatient

Allows patients to continue living at home while attending scheduled treatment sessions. Suitable for those with jobs or extensive social supports.

Day Treatment

Provides intensive services during the day while patients return home at night. Balances structure with flexibility.

Sober Living

Group homes for people recovering from addiction that provide a substance-free environment and peer support.

The Recovery Journey

1

Acknowledgment

Recognizing the problem and deciding to make a change is the first step toward recovery.

2

Seeking Help

Reaching out to healthcare providers, counselors, or treatment centers for professional guidance.

3

Detoxification

The process of clearing drugs from the body while managing withdrawal symptoms.

4

Active Treatment

Engaging in therapy, medication, and other treatments to address the root causes of addiction.

5

Maintenance

Building skills and support systems to maintain sobriety and prevent relapse.

6

Advanced Recovery

Living a balanced, healthy life and giving back to others who are still struggling.
